Lab 4

In an experiment, fifth grade students are individually and randomly assigned to be introduced to a set of vocabulary words by watching a lecture in one of three subject areas: physical science, social science, and history. The lecture was delivered to each child in one of two methods: traditional lecture or computer-augmented lecture. Six children experienced each combination of conditions. A vocabulary test with 60 words follows each lecture as a measure of learning.

Our research questions are:
1. To what extent do lecture topic and method influence the vocabulary learning of fifth grade students?
2. To what extent does the effect of lecture topic on the vocabulary learning of fifth grade students depend on lecture method?

Notes and Background

This is a 2-factor between subjects ANOVA, with lecture topic (physical science, social science, or history) and lecture delivery method (traditional lecture or computer-augmented lecture) as the two independent variables and vocabulary learning, determined by the score on the vocabulary test, as the dependent variable. The study is intended to represent the population of fifth graders subject to these conditions.

Computer-augmented lectures produce consistently higher vocabulary test means than standard lectures, regardless of subject area. Lab 4 APA Results

A two-way, between-subjects analysis of variance (ANOVA) was conducted to examine the extent to which instructional topic (physical science vs.social science vs.history) and lecture method (traditional vs.computer-assisted) affected the vocabulary learning of fifth graders. Results indicated that there was a significant interaction effect between instructional topic and lecture method (F(2, 30) = 6.49, p = .005, \(\omega^{2}\) = 0.234). In other words, the effect that lecture method has on students average vocabulary scores differs, or depends on, the type of lecture method.

Follow-up (i.e., post-hoc) simple effects were examined with a Tukeys HSD correction to interpret the interaction further, and found six statistically significant effects (p < .05). Specifically, when a lecture was delivered traditionally, students learned 22 more words on average in the physical science condition (M = 34) than the social science condition (M = 12), and a mean of 19 more words in the history condition (M = 31) than the social science condition (M = 12). In addition, in social science, students learned 28 more words on average if the lecture was computer-assisted (M = 40) as opposed to traditional lecture (M = 12).
